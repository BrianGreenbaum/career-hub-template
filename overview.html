Introducing

Career Hub

An AI-powered system for managing your job search, built on Claude Code

Brian Greenbaum · April 2026

The problem

Job searching is a full-time job

And you're doing most of it in your head.

  • Tracking applications across companies, stages, and contacts
  • Prepping for interviews with 20 years of work to draw from
  • Researching companies, teams, and the people you're talking to
  • Keeping your narrative sharp across dozens of conversations
  • All while probably still doing your current job

A different approach

Don't chat with AI.
Build a workspace it can operate in.

Instead of a single conversation that forgets everything, create a structured folder where AI reads your files, learns your context, and runs reusable workflows.

The old way

Open ChatGPT. Paste your resume. Ask a question. Close the tab. Start over next time.

This way

A folder of structured files that grows with you. The AI reads them every session. Nothing gets lost. Workflows are reusable.

Quick orientation

What is Claude Code?

An AI tool that works directly with files on your computer. Available as a CLI, desktop app, web app, and IDE extension.

  • It reads and writes files in your project folder
  • It reads CLAUDE.md files for instructions, so your folder teaches the AI about you
  • It can run reusable workflows called "skills"
  • It can connect to external services (Google, calendars, etc.)

The building blocks

Four concepts make this work

1

Folder structure

Organized files and folders give the AI structured context. Each folder has a purpose: job search, work history, networking, etc.

2

CLAUDE.md

Instruction files the AI reads automatically. They teach it who you are, what you're looking for, and how to help you.

3

Skills

Reusable workflows you invoke with /commands. Run a job search, do an interview, build a case study. Same quality every time.

4

MCPs

Connections to external services. Pull meeting notes from Granola, check your calendar, search the web. Bring outside data in.

How it works

The folder structure

CareerHub/
├── CLAUDE.md ← your profile & instructions
├── TODOS.md ← Kanban task board
├── Search/2026/ ← active job search
│   ├── DASHBOARD.md
│   ├── companies/
│   └── searches/
├── Work-History/ ← past roles
├── Weekly/ ← journal entries
├── Networking/ ← relationships
├── Portfolio/ ← case studies
└── .claude/skills/ ← reusable workflows

Each folder has a job

  • Search tracks your full pipeline: prospects, applications, interviews, offers
  • Work-History holds structured summaries of every role and project
  • Weekly captures recaps so you don't lose track of what's happening
  • Networking remembers the people you talk to and what you discussed

Works in Obsidian as a vault with Kanban for task management. Also works as plain markdown files.

The viewer

Obsidian is your IDE

A free markdown editor that treats your hub folder as a vault. Claude operates on the files regardless — Obsidian is how you read, navigate, and manage them day to day.

  • Point Obsidian at your Career Hub and the whole folder becomes a "vault"
  • Markdown renders with clickable links, backlinks, outlines, and a graph view
  • Navigate between companies, skills, and past roles without hunting through folders
  • Everything stays plain markdown — no lock-in, works in any editor
Install the Kanban plugin (by mgmeyers, from the community plugins browser) to turn TODOS.md into a drag-and-drop task board. More on that next.

Task management

One Kanban for everything

Action items across search, networking, and work all live on a single board at TODOS.md. Managed from the CLI, rendered as drag-and-drop in Obsidian.

How you write tasks

  • Columns: Not Started → Started → Waiting → Done → Archive
  • Tag by area: #search #networking #work
  • Prioritize with 🔴 High   🟡 Medium   🟢 Low
  • Add due dates with @{2026-04-25}

How you manage them

  • /todo add [task] — add a new card
  • /todo done [task] — mark complete
  • /todo move [task] [column] — change column
  • /todo list #search — filter by tag

What it does for you

Three pillars

The hub helps you in three distinct ways. Each one compounds over time as you feed it more context.

Second Brain

Captures everything in structured files so the AI has full context every session. Doesn't forget.

Coach

Prepares you for interviews and gives feedback grounded in your actual work history.

Research Assistants

Automates company research, people lookup, and exhaustive job searches across 12+ sources.

Pillar 1

Second Brain

  • Captures what you share — verbally, from documents, from meeting notes — into structured files
  • Builds a rich picture of your work history, search activity, contacts, and performance
  • Doesn't forget, because it takes structured notes in organized folders
  • Available to the AI in every session — full context, every time
The reflection bonus: When you do project interviews, you're not just training the AI. The act of storytelling and reflecting triggers memories and sharpens your narrative for real conversations.

Pillar 2

Coach

  • Helps you prep for "tell me about a time when..." questions
  • Gives feedback on your narrative, resume, and interview responses
  • Coaching is grounded in your actual history — not generic advice
  • Can role-play interviews and suggest stronger ways to frame your experience
Key insight: Because the AI has your full work history and project dossiers, its coaching is specific. It can say "remember when you led that migration at your last company?" instead of "consider mentioning a leadership example."

Pillar 3

Research Assistants

  • Runs exhaustive job searches across 12+ sources based on your criteria
  • Deep-dives on companies: AI strategy, leadership, culture, financials
  • Looks up people you're interviewing with — their background, interests, shared connections
  • Web research, your own data, and third-party tools — all in one place
The time savings are real: Research that would take hours happens in minutes. Bull/bear analysis on a company, compensation benchmarking, liquidity assessment — the grunt work gets automated so you can focus on the human parts.

Important framing

Be the best version of yourself

What this is

  • A system that keeps you organized and prepared
  • A way to extend yourself — do things you couldn't do alone
  • A tool for reflection that sharpens your real narrative
  • A research team that saves you hours every week

What this is not

  • A magic job-getting machine
  • A replacement for doing the actual work
  • A tool for making things up or embellishing
  • Something that removes the need for human judgment
The goal: authenticity at your best. This project helps you be organized, informed, and well-prepared — not fabricated.

Key skill

/onboard

Your first 10 minutes with the hub

  • A guided interview about your background, current role, and what you're looking for
  • Generates a customized CLAUDE.md with your profile, goals, and preferences

The bigger picture

A pattern for knowledge work, not just job search

The Career Hub is one instance of a broader idea: structured folder + skills + MCPs as a way for knowledge workers to interact with AI.

Instead of a single chat, a project, or building an app — you create a folder where you save files in a structured way, build skills for repeatable work, and use MCPs to pull in third-party data.

This is very similar to Dave Killeen's DEX system and other structured AI approaches. The pattern works because it gives AI context and memory without needing a custom app.

I use this pattern for:

Career

Job search, work history, networking, content

Life

Personal management, planning, journaling

Health

Health tracking, research, appointments

Make it yours

Treat this as a base, not a finished product

  • Add skills when you find yourself repeating work. If you do it twice, make it a skill.
  • Modify the structure for your situation. Delete what you don't use. Add folders that matter to you.
  • Edit CLAUDE.md whenever your situation changes — new role, new goals, new search criteria.
  • Build your own skills using the existing ones as examples. They're just markdown files.
How this was built: This system grew organically through trial and error over the course of a real job search. It's not perfect — it's extracted from what actually worked. Your version should evolve the same way.

Pro tips

Pair the hub with these tools

Two external tools that make the whole system work faster and capture more.

Voice-to-text (e.g. Wispr Flow)

Speed up /project-interview and /update by dictating your answers instead of typing. You'll say more, and the interview feels like a real conversation.

Meeting recorder (e.g. Granola)

Record your meetings and feed the transcripts into the hub. Networking calls, interviews, 1:1s — every conversation becomes structured context the AI can draw from later.
